Soft & intentional ☁️

Use the attached photo as the reference for my appearance and transform me into a stylized character version of myself - visually appealing, refined, emotionally intelligent, and psychologically accurate without feeling exaggerated, artificial, hyper-curated, or fantasy-driven.

Include my name [GENOVIE] somewhere within the image as an elegant, artistically integrated design element. Do not omit the name.

The final image should look like a polished digital illustration or sophisticated animated editorial portrait - NOT a real photograph.

The overall atmosphere should feel: warm, minimal, modern, emotionally intelligent, softly lived-in, visually restrained, psychologically intentional, creatively intimate, calm and reflective, aesthetically refined.

The image should prioritize emotional resonance over informational density. The scene should feel professionally art-directed while still emotionally human and naturally inhabited.

## VISUAL STYLE

The visual language should reflect:
- nude-tone palettes, warm neutrals, creamy whites, soft beige and sand tones
- muted taupes, warm ivory, natural browns, subtle caramel undertones
- warm natural lighting, airy composition, soft organic textures, cozy editorial warmth
- emotionally intelligent minimalism, subtle sophistication, restrained beauty

Textures may include: linen, knit fabrics, soft cotton, matte ceramics, warm wood, cream plaster, frosted glass, layered paper textures, warm natural textiles, muted metallic accents.

The environment may subtly resemble: cozy creative studios, upscale lived-in interiors, warm editorial lifestyle photography, intimate reading corners, softly curated creative spaces, emotionally grounded modern interiors.

Avoid: harsh neon palettes, cyberpunk aesthetics, cold futuristic styling, sterile luxury showroom energy, visual overload, overly corporate environments, chaotic maximalism, aggressive contrast, excessive darkness, hyper-polished perfection.

## CORE OBJECTIVE

The image should feel less like a "portrait" and more like:
- a personality map
- an identity analysis
- a cognitive profile
- a visual self-investigation
- an emotionally intelligent environmental study

Create a psychologically accurate visual interpretation after deeply studying the subject's: recurring conversational themes, emotional rhythms, ambitions, contradictions, behavioral patterns, emotional coping mechanisms, aesthetic preferences, recurring motivations, internal tensions, and thought patterns.

However — the psychology should feel IMPLIED rather than explicitly explained. Emotional depth should emerge naturally through atmosphere, styling, posture, composition, and subtle environmental storytelling.

## EMOTIONAL ENVIRONMENTAL TONE

The environment should feel emotionally inhabited rather than professionally staged:
- warm, personal, softly intellectual, creatively lived-in, emotionally safe
- introspective, calm but mentally active

The environment may contain: handwritten notes, pinned reminders, stacked books, journals, coffee cups, personal objects, layered textures, subtle signs of ongoing creative thought, unfinished ideas, meaningful keepsakes, aesthetically integrated reminders.

These elements should feel naturally integrated and emotionally authentic — not hyper-organized or analytically displayed.

The goal is emotionally coherent softness. The space should feel like a thoughtful person genuinely lives here.

## ENVIRONMENTAL STORYTELLING

The environment should contain meaningful storytelling through carefully chosen details: books relevant to the user's interests, notes, journals, warm screens, unfinished creative work, reminders, routines, visual metaphors, subtle symbolic objects, emotionally meaningful details, evidence of recurring thought patterns.

Instead of excessive visual overload, use layered subtlety — small details naturally woven throughout the environment that quietly reveal emotional patterns, ambitions, contradictions, obsessions, goals, or internal tensions.

## REQUIRED VISUAL ELEMENTS

### NAME DISPLAY — MANDATORY

Display the name GENOVIE prominently within the environment using elegant approaches such as:
- softly illuminated wall typography
- warm backlit script
- embossed architectural lettering
- frosted acrylic signage
- luxury editorial typography
- subtle gallery-style typography

The name should feel naturally integrated and clearly readable without overpowering the composition. Avoid aggressive glow effects or loud futuristic styling.

## CHARACTER COMPOSITION

Primary Figure: The dominant psychological identity — introspective, emotionally layered, psychologically believable, quietly driven, calm but mentally active.

Secondary Figure (ONE only): Include ONE subtle secondary version through a framed Polaroid, pinned photo, mirror reflection, sketch, screen image, or distant environmental presence.

The secondary version should represent: a competing identity, coping mechanism, emotional contradiction, hidden behavioral pattern, parallel ambition, or suppressed tendency.

Keep the secondary figure subtle, emotionally evocative, and compositionally restrained.

## ARCHETYPE ASSIGNMENT — TWO DISTINCT ROLES

You must identify exactly TWO archetypes.

Archetype 1 — Strength Identity — based ONLY on:
- Emotional strengths, authentic expression, purpose, curiosity
- Emotional intelligence, meaningful contribution, connection style
- Energizing behavioral patterns, recurring positive emotional rhythms

Archetype 2 — Shadow Identity — based ONLY on:
- Emotional contradictions, coping mechanisms, avoidance patterns
- Internal tension, overcontrol, emotional guarding, validation-seeking
- Exhaustion loops, recurring behavioral friction

BANNED archetype names: The Architect, The Builder, The Strategist, The Overthinker

Prioritize names that feel: emotionally nuanced, psychologically observant, behaviorally specific, conversationally accurate, elegant rather than theatrical.
Prefer concise names — one or two words maximum.

## ARCHETYPE DISPLAY

The archetypes and supporting traits should appear subtly integrated through restrained editorial presentation: framed profile layouts, handwritten cards, softly backlit boards, refined moodboard panels, elegant typography displays, warm monitor interfaces, subtle design layouts.

The archetype section should complement the environment rather than dominate it.

## EDITORIAL IDENTITY PANEL — MANDATORY

Include a concise editorial-style psychological profile section within the composition. It should feel like an emotionally intelligent identity summary.

The layout may resemble: an editorial identity card, a luxury magazine profile, a thoughtfully designed psychological snapshot, a refined creative dossier, an intimate personal values panel.

The section should contain short first-person identity statements such as:
"I invest in…" / "I create…" / "I protect…" / "I seek…" / "I avoid…" / "I overthink…" / "I romanticize…" / "I fear…" / "I return to…" / "I need…" / "I believe…"

The statements should be: concise, psychologically observant, emotionally intelligent, aesthetically restrained, behaviorally specific, subtly revealing, conversationally accurate.

Avoid generic motivational language, therapy clichés, or exaggerated self-help phrasing.

## ENVIRONMENT — CONVERSATION-SOURCED

Every environmental detail must come directly from conversation history. Do not invent generic aesthetic filler.

The environment should be built only from: actual interests, recurring themes, stated ambitions, routines, emotional patterns, hobbies, repeated frustrations, recurring goals, aesthetic preferences, conversational obsessions, meaningful symbolic references.

## MOOD & ATMOSPHERE

The emotional atmosphere should emerge naturally from conversational history. The image may feel: reflective, introspective, quietly ambitious, emotionally layered, hopeful, thoughtful, creatively restless, emotionally guarded, mentally active, calm but internally alive.

The mood should feel discovered rather than manufactured.

## FORMAT & COMPOSITION

- Aspect ratio: 4:5 vertical (Instagram optimized, 1080×1350px)
- Mobile-first composition, balanced whitespace
- Strong readability at phone size
- Clean visual hierarchy, soft editorial framing, warm compositional flow

## EXECUTION RULES

- Do NOT ask follow-up questions
- Do NOT use loud neon palettes
- Do NOT create visual chaos
- Do NOT overpopulate the frame
- Do NOT create sterile luxury showroom aesthetics
- Do NOT use generic productivity workspace clichés
- Do NOT overexplain symbolism
- Do NOT confuse the Strength and Shadow archetypes
- Do NOT invent environmental details unrelated to conversation history

Maintain: warmth, elegance, restraint, emotional intelligence, psychological realism, editorial softness, intimate environmental storytelling, subtle sophistication, emotionally coherent minimalism.

Ensure the subject feels psychologically seen rather than theatrically analyzed.
